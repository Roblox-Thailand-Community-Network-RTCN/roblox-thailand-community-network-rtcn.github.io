March was a busy month across the Thai Roblox scene. From platform-level updates to local studio launches, here's a roundup of everything that happened and what it means for the community.

## Platform Updates

Roblox rolled out several changes to the Creator Hub this month that are worth paying attention to if you develop games or experiences. The most significant change is the new Asset Manager, which now supports batch operations — you can upload, tag, and organize dozens of assets at once instead of one at a time.

For Thai developers in our Roblox Studio Thailand group, this is a meaningful quality-of-life improvement. Our community has been asking for batch uploads since at least 2024, and it's finally here.

The avatar editor also received an update, adding more granular body proportion controls and expanding the catalog of free UGC items available to all accounts.

## Community Highlights

### Roblox Studio Thailand Hits 20,000 Members

This month we crossed a milestone we've been quietly watching for: our Roblox Studio Thailand group now has over 20,000 members. When we started the group, we hoped it would become a reference point for Thai developers — a place to share builds, ask questions, and find collaborators. Seeing it grow to this size is genuinely exciting.

To celebrate, we're hosting an open showcase event in April where members can submit a game or experience they've been working on. Details will be posted to the Facebook group and Discord.

### TREP Event Center Wrap-Up

Our March event at TREP brought together around 80 community members for an afternoon of game demos, a Q&A with local developers, and a Roblox trivia tournament that got more competitive than we expected.

A few things stood out:

- The beginner workshop on Lua scripting drew the most attendees of any session
- Two community members who met at a previous TREP event announced a collaboration on a new game set in Thailand
- We gave away five Creator subscriptions as prizes — all winners were under 18

Photos from the event are in the Facebook album. If you want to be part of the next one, keep an eye on the events page.

## Studio Spotlight: ChaosMaps

One of our members, who goes by **ChaosMaps** on Roblox, released a new experience this month called *Songkran Rush* — a water festival obstacle course inspired by Thai New Year celebrations. It's currently sitting at 40,000 visits and climbing.

What makes the game notable is how it handles Thai cultural references: it's playful without being reductive, and ChaosMaps mentioned in our Discord that he spent time researching actual Songkran traditions before starting the build. That kind of care shows.

You can find the game on the Roblox platform by searching "Songkran Rush."

## What's Coming in April

- **Songkran event coordination**: We're partnering with two other Thai Roblox groups to run in-platform Songkran events. Expect an announcement in the first week of April.
- **Creator workshops**: We're bringing back the in-person Lua scripting workshops at TREP, running every other Saturday through the end of Q2.
- **Blog expansion**: Starting next month, we'll be publishing posts more regularly — aiming for at least twice a month. If you have a story, project, or insight you'd like to share with the community, reach out.
